🎉 What's New in v1.12
Stop orders, Trigger SL mode, faster Flatten All, critical alerts, slippage logging, and more. Tap any item below for details.

A third tab beside Standard and Conditional. Place breakout entries that fire when price touches your trigger level (industry-standard intra-tick behaviour — matches Topstep, NinjaTrader, IB, CME native stops).

  • Stop-Market: fires a market order when price crosses your trigger. Guaranteed fill, some slippage possible.
  • Stop-Limit: fires a limit order at trigger ± your offset. Price guaranteed, fill not guaranteed.
  • Buy Stop: trigger must be ABOVE current market.
  • Sell Stop: trigger must be BELOW current market.
  • SL and TPs attach automatically on fill, just like any other entry.

A third SL mode (alongside Simple and Smart) that moves your stop loss based on price distance from entry, not TP fills.

  • Example: "When price travels +36 ticks from entry → move SL to entry + 2 ticks."
  • Uses the master TICKS/POINTS unit selector.
  • Optional Smart after TP checkbox: after the trigger fires AND your chosen TP fills, the SL also follows Smart-style stepping (step to previous TP price on each subsequent fill).
  • Solves the classic "lock BE at +X ticks regardless of TPs" workflow that's common with Topstep / prop-firm traders.

The Conditional Order tab now has a Type selector. Limit (original behaviour) places a limit at the trigger on candle close. Market places a market order on candle close — useful for breakouts where guaranteed fill matters more than exact price.

If a safety-critical operation fails after retries — such as moving your SL to breakeven, or placing the initial SL — you'll get all three at once:

  • 🚨 Modal popup that stays on top until you dismiss it
  • System beep
  • Discord webhook ping (also forwarded to the dev team if persistent)

Normal trades are silent. Alerts only fire on real failures so you never miss a position that's been left unprotected.

When a Stop or Conditional entry fires, the log now shows realised slippage in this format:

SLIPPAGE [STOP-MARKET] #12345: trigger 20000.00 → fill 20002.50 (+10.0 ticks, worse)

Positive ticks = worse than trigger (paid more on buy, got less on sell). Negative = better fill. exact = no slippage. Useful for measuring fast vs slow market behaviour over time.

Flatten All used to cancel orders sequentially before sending the market close — typically 2-3 seconds while price moved against you. Now the market close fires in parallel with the order cancels, so the position usually closes within ~300ms of clicking. Critical for prop traders bleeding profit on slow flattens.

The last instrument you traded is restored automatically on next launch (matched by contract ID — survives Topstep's monthly contract roll). The window's position and size are also restored, with a guard against restoring to a monitor that has since been disconnected.

Take Profits are arranged in two columns of three (TP1-3 col 1, TP4-6 col 2). If you need more than 6 levels, an Advanced TP Setup dialog opens for the extras — keeps the main UI compact so FLATTEN ALL and EMERGENCY BE stay visible at all times.

3. Ticks vs Points

The TICKS / POINTS toggle at the top of the preset card sets the master unit for stop loss, take profits, runner trail, and the Trigger SL value.

The conversion uses the current contract's tick size. For NQ futures, 1 point = 4 ticks (tick size 0.25); for ES, 1 point = 4 ticks (tick size 0.25).

💡 Switching the toggle automatically converts the displayed values — your underlying SL/TP prices stay the same.

5. Runner (Trailing Stop)

The Runner converts your last TP into a trailing stop instead of a fixed-price exit. Useful when you want to ride extended moves without giving back too much.

● RunnerClick to enable. The last TP becomes a trailing stop.
Trail (pts/ticks)Distance the stop follows behind the highest favourable price
Every (sec)How often the runner checks for a new high (minimum 1 second)
💡 Runner needs network connectivity to track price. If your internet drops, the runner pauses but your fixed SL still protects you.

6. Stop Loss

The STOP LOSS field sets the distance from entry at which the protective stop sits. The value is in the master unit (ticks or points).

TradePilot uses a single shared SL per contract+side (one SL covers all open contracts in that direction). This is intentional for rate-limit efficiency. To stagger SL behaviour, use Smart mode with multiple TPs — the SL tightens as TPs fill.

7. SL Modes — Simple, Smart, Trigger

Simple

After N take profits fill (you choose N), the SL moves to breakeven plus your offset. Stays there for the rest of the trade.

Smart

Same BE trigger as Simple, but then keeps stepping. Each subsequent TP fill moves the SL to the previous TP's fill price — locking in more profit progressively.

Trigger NEW in v1.12

SL moves when price travels a specified distance from entry, independent of TPs.

  • Trigger: distance from entry (in your master unit) that fires the SL move. Example: 36 ticks.
  • Offset: where the SL settles when fired (entry + offset). Example: 2 ticks past entry = small fee buffer.
  • Smart after TP (optional): after the trigger fires AND the chosen TP fills, SL also follows Smart-style stepping for subsequent fills.
💡 Trigger fires once per trade. The SL never moves backward once locked in.

9. Critical Alerts NEW in v1.12

For safety-critical failures — such as the SL→BE move not going through, or the initial SL being rejected by the exchange — TradePilot fires three alerts at once:

  • Modal popup on top of all other windows until dismissed
  • System beep
  • Discord webhook ping (also forwarded to the dev team)

Each operation retries automatically with exponential backoff (200ms → 2s) before an alert fires. If the initial SL placement fails after retries, TradePilot also emergency-closes the position at market so you're never left unprotected.

⚠ Alerts are intentionally loud. If you ever see one mid-trade, check your position immediately.

12. Troubleshooting

Connection Lost Banner

If the red "Connection lost" banner appears, TradePilot cannot reach the TopstepX API. It will automatically retry. If it persists for more than 30 seconds, restart TradePilot.

Status Dot Yellow

This may indicate rate limiting. TradePilot automatically enters degraded mode and retries with backoff. Avoid placing rapid orders during this time.

"Price not available" on OFFSET

The TopstepX / ProjectX API occasionally fails to return a price quote. OFFSET mode needs a current price to calculate the limit. Wait a few seconds and retry, or switch to PRICE mode and type the limit directly.

Order Blocked

TradePilot blocks opposite-direction orders on the same contract (e.g. buying while a sell bracket is active). Close the existing bracket first.

Critical Alert Popup Appeared

One of your safety-critical operations failed after retries. Check the popup message — it tells you exactly which order and which contract. Verify your position on TopstepX immediately. The alert is also forwarded to the dev team via Discord.

Debug Log

Detailed logs are saved to ~/.tradepilot/tradepilot_debug.log. Click the 🐛 button to send the last 100 lines to the dev team (credentials are automatically stripped).

Config Location

Your settings are stored in ~/.tradepilot/config.json. This persists across updates. If you need a fresh start, delete the ~/.tradepilot/ folder.
