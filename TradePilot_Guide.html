1. Getting Started

Launch TradePilot and enter your TopstepX credentials:

UsernameYour TopstepX email address

DRY RUN mode lets you explore the interface without connecting to TopstepX. No orders are placed.

2. Presets

3. Ticks vs Points

Toggle between TICKS and POINTS to set how values are interpreted.

TicksMinimum price increment. NQ: 1 tick = 0.25 points. ES: 1 tick = 0.25 points.
PointsFull price units. NQ: 1 point = 4 ticks. ES: 1 point = 4 ticks.

The info label below the toggle shows the conversion: e.g. tick=0.25 1pt=4tk

⚠ Points mode on non-index futures (CL, GC) may give unexpected values. Use Ticks mode for these instruments.

4. Take Profits

Each TP level has a distance (in ticks or points) and a contract count.

TP1, TP2, TP3...Take profit levels. Distance is from your entry price.
× contractsHow many contracts close at this TP level
+ / −Add or remove TP levels (minimum 1)
Total ctSum of all TP contracts — this is your entry order size

Example: TP1 = 12 ticks × 2 ct, TP2 = 24 ticks × 1 ct, TP3 = 48 ticks × 1 ct = 4 contracts total entry.

5. Runner (Trailing Stop)

The Runner converts your last TP into a trailing stop. Instead of closing at a fixed price, it follows the market and locks in profit as price moves in your favor.

● RunnerClick to enable/disable. Green dot = active, grey = off.
TrailHow far behind the highest price the stop follows. Smaller = tighter trail, locks profit faster but may get stopped out on pullbacks.
Every ___ secondsHow often the runner checks for a new high to move the trailing stop. Lower = more responsive but uses more API calls. Minimum 1 second.
⚠ When Runner is enabled, the last TP's distance is ignored — only its contract count is used. The stop trails instead of taking profit at a fixed level.
💡 A good starting trail for NQ is 10-20 ticks. For ES, 8-16 ticks. Adjust based on your timeframe.

6. Stop Loss

The stop loss distance from your entry price. Applied to ALL contracts in the bracket.

Stop LossDistance in ticks or points. Must be a positive number.

The stop is placed as a STOP order immediately after your entry fills. If the exchange rejects the stop (e.g. velocity controls), TradePilot immediately executes an emergency market close — you won't be left with an unprotected position.

7. SL Modes — Simple vs Smart

Simple

Moves the stop loss to breakeven (your entry price + offset) after a set number of TPs fill.

BE after TPHow many TPs must fill before the stop moves to breakeven. Example: "2" means after TP1 and TP2 fill.
OffsetExtra ticks added above/below breakeven to cover commissions and fees. 0 = exact entry price. 2 = entry + 2 ticks of profit locked in.

Smart

Same as Simple for the first move (breakeven + offset), then steps the stop loss to each previous TP price as subsequent TPs fill.

Example: TP1 fills → nothing happens. TP2 fills → SL moves to breakeven + offset. TP3 fills → SL moves to TP2's price (no offset on steps, only on initial BE).

💡 Smart mode lets you lock in progressively more profit as each TP fills, without manual intervention.

8. Placing Orders

Limit Orders

OFFSET / PRICEToggle between offset mode (distance from market) and direct price mode
Input fieldIn OFFSET mode: ticks/points below market (buy) or above market (sell). In PRICE mode: exact limit price.

LIMIT BUY places a buy limit order below the current market price.

LIMIT SELL places a sell limit order above the current market price.

Market Orders

MARKET BUY and MARKET SELL execute immediately at the current market price.

⚠ There is a 2-second cooldown between order clicks to prevent accidental double orders.
💡 Right-click the offset/price field to paste from clipboard (auto-validates numeric input).

9. Flatten & Emergency BE

FLATTEN ALLCancels ALL open orders, then closes ALL open positions with market orders. Use in emergencies.
⚡ EMERGENCY BEMoves ALL active stop losses to breakeven immediately, regardless of how many TPs have filled.
⚠ FLATTEN ALL is irreversible — it closes everything. A confirmation dialog + 5-second cooldown prevents accidental clicks.

11. Troubleshooting

Connection Lost Banner

If the red "Connection lost" banner appears, TradePilot cannot reach the TopstepX API. It will automatically retry. If it persists for more than 30 seconds, restart TradePilot.

Status Dot Yellow

This may indicate rate limiting. TradePilot automatically enters degraded mode to recover. Avoid placing rapid orders during this time.

Order Blocked

TradePilot blocks opposite-direction orders on the same contract (e.g. buying while a sell bracket is active). Close the existing bracket first.

Debug Log

Detailed logs are saved to ~/.tradepilot/tradepilot_debug.log. Click the 🐛 button to send the last 100 lines to the dev team (credentials are automatically stripped).

Config Location

Your settings are stored in ~/.tradepilot/config.json. This persists across updates. If you need a fresh start, delete the ~/.tradepilot/ folder.
